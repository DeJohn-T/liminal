A note from the other side

DJ,

I need you to sit with this for a second.

You grew up in Fillmore thinking the world was peaches and cream, not yet knowing what it cost to keep the lights on. You watched mom stretch one income across the four of us and never once let you feel poor. You didn't understand what "be better than us" meant yet but how could you? You were just a kid.

High school almost took you out. You were lost, bro. Truly lost. And you still showed up every day.

Then, in junior year, a teacher saw something in you that you couldn't see in yourself. One nomination changed the trajectory. Computer Science Courses, Dual Enrollment. UCSF. AP Chemistry. California Youth of the Year. $169,000 in scholarships. 16 college acceptances. First in the family to go.

And then you thought you had it figured out.

You didn't. Nobody does at 18, man. It literally happens to the best of us.

TSU broke you down in ways you didn't expect. You built college up in your head for years, the friends, the energy, the feeling of belonging somewhere, and instead you got thirty minutes from campus, an unfamiliar environment, no connections, no resources, and a silence so loud it followed you everywhere. You were performing okay on the outside and falling apart on the inside. You've been good at that since you were 13.

But here's what you did: you didn't stay. You made a decision. You convinced yourself to move, to try again, to bet on yourself one more time when you had every reason not to. That took something that doesn't have a name.

LSU gave you breathing room. Friends. A real campus. Motivation. Resources. A challenge. Something that felt like a life. And then 2023 hit. ADHD undiagnosed and untreated, barely enough money to function — and somewhere underneath all of it, you still didn't know who you were supposed to be. Depression came back, heavier this time. You genuinely thought this was the end game not gonna lie.

I know.

But bro, you're here. Reading this. From Spain. No joke.

Think about that for a second. The kid from Fillmore. Bro you've been in trouble I can't fathom, been in life threatning situations as a kid, almost didn't make it through sophomore year, broke down in a city where nobody knew your name, not knowing how to ask for help. That kid is living in Madrid right now. Walking through cities his parents have never seen. That his family has never seen. Making friends from Brazil to Australia and everywhere in between. Standing on the top of the Eiffel Tower on Christmas Eve. Not because everything went right. Because it didn't, not at all. It's because he kept going when everything went wrong.

You are 1 of 4 out of hundreds who got to do this. You did it. With barely any money, while feeling like the world is caving in, with every setback that would have been a legitimate reason to stop. You didn't stop.

Our parents didn't finish high school. But dude. You are in college, debt-free from the jump, on your way into tech, with a dream internship, backed with love, with your camera full of five months of a life most people only imagine. You are not the statistics, bro. You never were. You are him.

I won't tell you it gets easy. You know better than that by now. But I will tell you this — every time life has gotten heavy, something in you has found a way to move. That's not luck. It's commendable. It's you. It was always you.

The light was never somewhere out there.

It was you the whole time.

Never stop pushing kid. You'll do amazing things. Change the world.

— DJ. Later. 22 years old. Nearly 7 years in the future.
